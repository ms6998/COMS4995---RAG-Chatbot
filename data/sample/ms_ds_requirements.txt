MS Data Science - Degree Requirements (2023 Catalog)

Program Overview:
The Master of Science in Data Science is a 30-credit program that combines statistical methodology, computational thinking, and real-world applications. The program prepares students for careers as data scientists in industry, government, and academia.

Core Courses (15 credits):
All students must complete the following FIVE core courses:

1. COMS 4721 Machine Learning for Data Science (3 credits)
   Introduction to supervised and unsupervised learning methods

2. STAT 5702 Exploratory Data Analysis and Visualization (3 credits)
   Statistical methods and visualization techniques

3. COMS 4995 Applied Machine Learning (3 credits)
   Practical implementation of ML algorithms

4. IEOR 4540 Data Mining (3 credits)
   Techniques for discovering patterns in large datasets

5. STAT 5703 Statistical Inference and Modeling (3 credits)
   Foundation in statistical theory and methods

Elective Requirements (12 credits):
Students must complete 12 credits of electives chosen from approved courses in:
- Computer Science (COMS)
- Statistics (STAT)
- Industrial Engineering (IEOR)
- Electrical Engineering (ELEN)

Recommended Electives:
Computer Science:
- COMS 4111 Introduction to Databases
- COMS 4705 Natural Language Processing
- COMS 4771 Machine Learning
- COMS 4995 Deep Learning

Statistics:
- STAT 5704 Linear Regression Models
- STAT 5705 Generalized Linear Models
- STAT 5706 Time Series Analysis

Industrial Engineering:
- IEOR 4510 Project Management
- IEOR 4650 Business Analytics
- IEOR 4742 Deep Learning

At least 6 credits must be at the 6000-level or above.

Capstone Project (3 credits):
All students must complete a capstone project:
- STAT 5293 Data Science Capstone (3 credits)

The capstone involves:
- Working with real-world data from industry partners
- Team-based project (3-4 students)
- Final presentation and written report
- Completed in final semester

Prerequisites:
Students should have background in:
- Calculus and Linear Algebra (equivalent to MATH 1201-1202, MATH 2010)
- Probability and Statistics (STAT 1001 or equivalent)
- Programming (Python preferred, or R, Java, C++)
- Data Structures (helpful but not required)

Deficiency Courses:
If prerequisites are not met, students may need to take:
- STAT 4001 Introduction to Probability
- COMS 4156 Data Structures in Python
- MATH 2015 Linear Algebra and Applications

These do NOT count toward the 30-credit requirement.

Tracks:
Students can specialize in one of three tracks:

Computational Track:
Focus on algorithms, systems, and scalability
Required courses:
- COMS 4111 Databases
- COMS 4121 Computer Systems for Data Science
- Plus 2 computational electives

Statistical Track:
Focus on statistical theory and inference
Required courses:
- STAT 5704 Linear Regression
- STAT 5705 Generalized Linear Models
- Plus 2 statistical electives

Application Track:
Focus on domain-specific applications
Required courses:
- Choose domain: Finance, Healthcare, Social Science, etc.
- Complete 4 electives in chosen domain
- Examples: Financial Analytics, Health Analytics, Text Analytics

Academic Requirements:
- Minimum cumulative GPA: 3.0
- No more than one grade below B- in core courses
- All courses must be taken for a letter grade (no pass/fail)

Program Policies:

Time to Completion:
- Full-time: 3-4 semesters (18 months typical)
- Part-time: Up to 5 years

Course Load:
- Full-time: 9-15 credits per semester
- Part-time: 6-8 credits per semester
- Summer: Optional, 3-6 credits

Transfer Credits:
- Maximum 6 credits may be transferred
- Must be graduate-level courses
- Grade of B or better required
- Requires departmental approval

Academic Standing:
Students must maintain:
- Cumulative GPA ≥ 3.0
- Satisfactory academic progress
- Completion of core courses within first two semesters

Professional Development:
All students are encouraged to:
- Attend Data Science Institute seminars
- Participate in industry networking events
- Complete internship (optional, summer between years)
- Join data science student organizations

Graduation Requirements Summary:
☑ 30 credits total
☑ 15 credits of core courses (all 5 required)
☑ 12 credits of electives (6 at 6000-level)
☑ 3 credits capstone project
☑ Cumulative GPA ≥ 3.0
☑ Completed within time limit
