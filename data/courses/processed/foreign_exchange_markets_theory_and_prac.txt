TITLE: Foreign Exchange Markets Theory and Prac
SUBJECT: Intl Finance & Economic Policy
INSTRUCTOR: Lisa M Chung
DESCRIPTION: This course provides an in-depth examination of the foreign exchange (FX) marketâ€”the largest and most liquid financial market in the world. Students will explore the structure of the FX market, key market participants, and trading conventions. Through a combination of theory, practical tools, and analysis of current market developments, the course offers insights into how currencies are traded, valued, and influenced by macroeconomic policy and geopolitical trends. Topics include currency movements and valuation, FX market structure, trading practices, U.S. dollar dominance, FX intervention by central banks, and reserve management strategies. Guest speakers from both the public and private sectors will provide professional perspectives, and students will engage in topical discussions linking theory to real-world events.
